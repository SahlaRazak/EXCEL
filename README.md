# Healthcare Data Analysis and Insights

## OBJECTIVE
To analyze a comprehensive healthcare dataset by performing data cleaning, transformation, and exploratory analysis in order to uncover patterns in patient health, medical histories, and healthcare costs, 
enabling data-driven decision-making for healthcare providers and policymakers.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Pivot Tables, Slicers, and Charts

## Health Care Dashboard Features
1. Data Filtering & Slicing
    Diabetes Status Filter: Allows users to segment data by Diabetes, Normal, or Pre-Diabetes.
    Weight Status Filter: Enables specific views for Obesity, Overweight, Normal Weight, and Underweight categories.

2. Financial Analysis
    Cost vs. Condition: Tracks total healthcare charges based on weight status and diabetic conditions.
    Age-Based Spending: Visualizes how healthcare costs fluctuate across different age brackets (11-70).

3. Surgical & Transplant Tracking
    Surgery Tier Breakdown: A stacked bar chart identifying surgery intensity across three different tiers.
    Transplant History Ratio: A pie chart illustrating the percentage of patients who have undergone transplants versus those who have not.

4. Health Metric Correlations
    BMI & HbA1C Trends: A line graph comparing Body Mass Index (BMI) and blood sugar levels (HbA1C) across age groups to find correlations.
    Cancer & Lifestyle: A donut chart showing the distribution of cancer history among smokers and non-smokers.

5. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
    Risk Identification: Highlights that obesity and the 51-60 age demographic represent the highest financial and clinical risk.
    Patient Demographics: Provides a numeric count of specific health histories (e.g., 1,847 negative vs. 488 positive cancer history cases).

 ## Key Skills Demonstrated
- Excel-based analytics  
- Data cleaning and transformation  
- Dashboard design and interactivity  
- Data storytelling for decision-making
